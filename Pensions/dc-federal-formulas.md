# D.C. & Federal Pension Formulas

All federal defined benefit pensions use the same fundamental approach: multiply a salary average by years of service and a benefit factor. The specific formula depends on which system covers you, when you were hired, and whether you hold a special-category position.

## FERS Formulas

All FERS formulas use the same core equation:

> **High-3 Average Salary × Years of Creditable Service × Multiplier = Annual Pension**

The three variables:
- **High-3 Average Salary** — the <a href="https://www.planwellfp.com/federal-retirement-high-3-how-to-calculate">highest average basic pay earned during any 3 consecutive years of service</a> (36-month moving average). Includes base pay + locality pay; excludes overtime, bonuses, and premium pay.
- **Years of Creditable Service** — total years and partial years of qualifying federal employment, including unused sick leave credit at retirement
- **Multiplier** — either 1.0% or 1.1%, depending on your age and service at retirement

### Standard FERS Formula

| Condition | Multiplier |
|-----------|-----------|
| Retire **before** age 62, or with **less than** 20 years of service | **1.0%** |
| Retire at age **62 or older** with **20+ years** of service | **1.1%** |

* see also: <a href="https://www.opm.gov/retirement-center/fers-information/computation/">OPM — FERS Computation</a>
* see also: <a href="https://www.fedweek.com/ask/federal-retirement/calculating-federal-annuity/">FedWeek — Calculating a Federal Annuity</a>

### FERS Special Provisions (Law Enforcement, Firefighters, Air Traffic Controllers)

These positions receive an enhanced formula and earlier retirement eligibility. Eligible employees can retire at **age 50 with 20 years of covered service** or **any age with 25 years** and face **mandatory retirement at age 57** (with possible extension to 60 by agency head).

| Service | Multiplier |
|---------|-----------|
| First 20 years of covered LEO/Firefighter/ATC service | **1.7%** |
| Each year beyond 20 | **1.0%** |

* see also: <a href="https://www.capitalwealth.com/services/federal-benefits/fers-calculator">Capital Wealth — FERS Calculator (Special Provisions)</a>
* see also: <a href="https://www.fedweek.com/ask/federal-retirement/retirement-eligibility/">FedWeek — Special Category Retirement</a>

### FERS Congressional Formula

Members of Congress and certain congressional staff (first covered before 2013) receive an enhanced formula:

| Service | Multiplier |
|---------|-----------|
| First 20 years of Congressional service | **1.7%** |
| Each year beyond 20 | **1.0%** |

Members and staff first covered by FERS in 2013 or later accrue benefits at the standard 1.0%/1.1% rate.

* see also: <a href="https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R47084">CRS Report R47084 — Congressional Retirement</a>

### MRA+10 Early Retirement Reduction

If you retire at your MRA with at least 10 years (but fewer than 30) of service, your annuity is permanently reduced by **5% for each year you are under age 62**. You can eliminate this penalty by deferring (postponing) the start of your annuity to age 62.

* see also: <a href="https://www.opm.gov/frequently-asked-questions/retire-faq/pre-retirement/what-is-a-minimum-retirement-age-mra-plus-10-annuity-under-the-federal-employees-retirement-system-fers/">OPM — MRA+10 Annuity</a>

## Contribution Rates

Pensions are funded by employee contributions, agency/employer contributions, and investment returns. The employer side is invisible on your paycheck but represents an enormous portion of total compensation.

### FERS Contributions (Employee + Agency)

**Employee contribution rates (toward FERS Basic Annuity):**

| Tier | Employee Rate | Agency Rate (Approximate) | Source |
|------|-------------|--------------------------|--------|
| **FERS** (hired before 1/1/2013) | 0.8% | ~17.3% | <a href="https://plan-your-federal-retirement.com/fers-contributions/">Plan Your Federal Retirement — FERS Contributions</a> |
| **FERS-RAE** (hired 2013) | 3.1% | ~15.0% | <a href="https://www.federalpensionadvisors.com/post/federal-employee-retirement-contributions">Federal Pension Advisors — Contribution Guide</a> |
| **FERS-FRAE** (hired 2014+) | 4.4% | ~10.9% | <a href="https://www.federalpensionadvisors.com/post/federal-employee-retirement-contributions">Federal Pension Advisors — Contribution Guide</a> |
| **CSRS** | 7.0% | 7.0% (matching) | <a href="https://www.opm.gov/retirement-center/csrs-information/">OPM — CSRS Information</a> |
| **LEO/Firefighter/ATC** (add to tier rate) | +0.5% | Higher (special provision normal cost) | <a href="https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/04/14/2023-07876/federal-employees-retirement-system-normal-cost-percentages">OPM — FERS Normal Cost Percentages (Federal Register)</a> |

Agency contribution rates are determined by OPM based on actuarial valuations and published as <a href="https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/04/14/2023-07876/federal-employees-retirement-system-normal-cost-percentages">normal cost percentages in the Federal Register</a>. Rates vary by employee category and are generally higher for special-provision employees.

**In addition to the pension contribution**, your agency also pays: 6.2% of your salary toward Social Security (OASDI), 1.45% toward Medicare, and up to 5% of salary in TSP matching — bringing total employer retirement contributions to roughly **25–30% of your salary** for FERS employees.

## FERS Worked Example Calculations

### Example 1: Standard FERS — GS-13 Engineer, Age 62, 30 Years

> **Maria** is a GS-13, Step 10 civil engineer at the Army Corps of Engineers in Washington, D.C. She retires at age 62 with 30 years of creditable service. Her high-3 average salary is $138,000.

**Formula:** High-3 × Years × 1.1% (age 62+ with 20+ years)

$138,000 × 30 × 0.011 = **$45,540 per year** ($3,795/month)

That's about **33% of her final salary** from the pension alone, before Social Security and TSP.

* see also: <a href="https://www.planwellfp.com/fers-retirement-calculator">PlanWell — FERS Retirement Calculator</a>

### Example 2: Standard FERS — GS-12 Analyst, Age 57 (MRA), 30 Years

> **James** is a GS-12 budget analyst who started at age 27. He retires at his MRA of 57 with 30 years of service. His high-3 is $112,000.

**Formula:** High-3 × Years × 1.0% (under 62)

$112,000 × 30 × 0.01 = **$33,600 per year** ($2,800/month)

No age reduction because he has 30 years at MRA. He also qualifies for the **FERS Special Retirement Supplement** (an estimated Social Security bridge payment) until age 62.

### Example 3: FERS Special Provision — Federal Law Enforcement Officer, Age 50, 25 Years

> **Sergeant Davis** is a federal law enforcement officer retiring at age 50 with 25 years of covered service. His high-3 is $125,000.

**Formula:** (High-3 × 20 years × 1.7%) + (High-3 × 5 years × 1.0%)

($125,000 × 20 × 0.017) + ($125,000 × 5 × 0.01) = $42,500 + $6,250 = **$48,750 per year** ($4,062.50/month)

That's about **39% of his final salary** — significantly more than the standard formula for the same years of service.

### Example 4: MRA+10 Early Retirement with Reduction

> **Karen** is age 57 (her MRA) with only 15 years of service. She wants to retire now. Her high-3 is $95,000.

**Formula:** High-3 × Years × 1.0% minus 5% per year under 62

$95,000 × 15 × 0.01 = $14,250 per year (unreduced)

Age penalty: 5 years under 62 × 5% = 25% reduction

$14,250 × 0.75 = **$10,687.50 per year** ($890.63/month)

Karen could alternatively **postpone** her annuity to age 62 to eliminate the reduction entirely.

## CSRS Formula (Closed System)

CSRS uses a **weighted three-tier formula** that rewards long careers. CSRS employees do **not** participate in Social Security (unless they also have sufficient private-sector earnings) and do **not** receive TSP agency matching contributions.

> **1.5% × High-3 × (first 5 years) + 1.75% × High-3 × (next 5 years) + 2.0% × High-3 × (years beyond 10)**

| Service Band | Multiplier |
|-------------|-----------|
| First 5 years | 1.5% per year |
| Years 5–10 | 1.75% per year |
| Years beyond 10 | 2.0% per year |

Maximum benefit: **80% of high-3** (reached at approximately 41 years and 11 months of service).

CSRS employees who retire under age 55 have their annuities reduced by 1/6 of 1% for each month under 55 (2% per year) — though this is now rare since the system closed in 1984.

* see also: <a href="https://www.fedweek.com/ask/federal-retirement/calculating-federal-annuity/">FedWeek — Computing a Federal Annuity – CSRS</a>
* see also: <a href="https://www.opm.gov/retirement-center/csrs-information/">OPM — CSRS Information</a>

### CSRS Worked Example

> **Robert**, a CSRS employee, retires at age 58 with 30 years of service and a high-3 of $100,000.

(1.5% × 5 × $100,000) + (1.75% × 5 × $100,000) + (2.0% × 20 × $100,000)

= $7,500 + $8,750 + $40,000 = **$56,250 per year** (56.25% of high-3)

## Foreign Service Formulas

The Foreign Service operates parallel retirement systems mirroring CSRS and FERS:

| System | Applies To | Basic Formula | Employee Contribution |
|--------|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| **FSRDS** | FSOs hired before 1984 | <a href="https://fam.state.gov/fam/03fam/03fam6110.html">2.0% × high-3 × years of service</a> | 7.25% |
| **FSPS** | FSOs hired after 12/31/1983 | <a href="https://fam.state.gov/fam/03fam/03fam6110.html">1.7% × high-3 × first 20 years + 1.0% × remaining years</a> (age 50+ with 20 years) | 1.35% |

FSPS participants also receive Social Security and TSP agency matching — structurally similar to FERS but with an enhanced formula reflecting the demanding nature of foreign service careers.

* see also: <a href="https://fam.state.gov/fam/03fam/03fam6110.html">3 FAM 6110 — Foreign Service Retirement: General</a>

## COLA — Cost-of-Living Adjustments

CSRS and FERS retirees receive different COLA treatment:

| CPI-W Change | CSRS COLA | FERS COLA |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| 2% or less | Full CPI-W increase | Full CPI-W increase |
| Between 2% and 3% | Full CPI-W increase | **Capped at 2.0%** |
| Over 3% | Full CPI-W increase | **CPI-W minus 1 percentage point** |

FERS COLAs are sometimes called **"diet COLAs"** because they fall behind the full CPI adjustment when inflation exceeds 2%.

Additionally, **FERS retirees under age 62 generally do not receive COLAs** on their basic annuity — with exceptions for disability annuitants, survivor annuitants, and special-provision retirees (LEO, firefighter, ATC).

CSRS COLAs have no age restriction.

D.C. local pension COLAs work differently — see the D.C. formulas section below.

* see also: <a href="https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF12354">CRS — The FERS "Diet COLA" and the Equal COLA Act</a>

## D.C. Local Pension Formulas

### D.C. Teachers' Retirement Plan

The formula depends on when you were hired:

**Hired before November 1, 1996:**

> (Average Salary × 1.5% × first 5 years) + (Average Salary × 1.75% × years 5–10) + (Average Salary × 2.0% × years beyond 10) = Annual Pension

**Hired on or after November 1, 1996:**

> **Average Salary × 2.0% × Years of Service = Annual Pension**

| Feature | Pre-11/1/1996 Hires | Post-11/1/1996 Hires |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Formula | Weighted (1.5%/1.75%/2.0%) | Flat 2.0% per year |
| Employee contribution | <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/page/summary-plan-faqs-retired-teachers">7% of salary</a> | <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/page/summary-plan-faqs-retired-teachers">8% of salary</a> |
| Average salary period | <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/service/glossary-benefit-terms-teachers">Highest 36 consecutive months</a> | Highest 36 consecutive months |
| Vesting | 5 years | 5 years |
| COLA cap | No cap (full CPI) | <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/release/2026-cost-living-adjustment-notices-washington-dc-public-school-teachers-police-officers-and">3.0% cap</a> |
| Social Security | Not covered | Not covered |

Voluntary retirement eligibility (post-11/1/1996): age 55 with 30 years, age 60 with 20 years, age 62 with 5 years, or any age with 30 years.

* see also: <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/page/summary-plan-faqs-retired-teachers">DCRB — Teacher Retirement FAQs</a>
* see also: <a href="https://home.treasury.gov/system/files/241/SPD-Teachers-Plan-2023.pdf">D.C. Teachers' Retirement Plan SPD 2023 (PDF)</a>
* see also: <a href="https://www.teacherpensions.org/state/district-columbia">TeacherPensions.org — D.C. State Analysis</a>
* see also: <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/service/retirement-benefits-calculator">DCRB Retirement Benefits Calculator</a>

### D.C. Police Officers' and Firefighters' Retirement Plan

The Police/Fire plan uses a flat-rate formula and allows retirement at **any age with 25 years of service** — one of the most generous public safety retirement provisions in the country.

> **Average Base Pay × 2.5% × Years of Creditable Service = Annual Pension**

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Formula | <a href="https://joinmpd.dc.gov/metropolitan-police/police-officer">2.5% × highest consecutive 3-year average base pay × years of service</a> |
| Maximum benefit | 80% of average base pay |
| Minimum retirement | Any age with 25 years of police/fire service |
| Alternative | Age 50 with 20 years (with longevity adjustments depending on tier) |
| Employee contribution | 7% (Tier 1/2) or 8% (Tier 3, hired after 11/10/1996) |
| COLA | Full CPI for pre-11/10/1996 hires; capped at 3% for later hires |
| Social Security | Not covered |

Tiers: The Police/Fire plan has three tiers based on hire and retirement date — Tier 1 (retired before 2/15/1980, receives equalization pay), Tier 2 (retired on/after 2/15/1980), and Tier 3 (hired on/after 11/10/1996).

* see also: <a href="https://joinmpd.dc.gov/metropolitan-police/police-officer">JoinMPD — Police Officer Benefits</a>
* see also: <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/page/summary-plans-faqs-retired-police-and-firefighters">DCRB — Police & Firefighter Retirement FAQs</a>
* see also: <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/service/glossary-benefit-terms-police-officers-and-firefighters">DCRB — Glossary of Terms for Police & Firefighters</a>
* see also: <a href="https://dcrb.dc.gov/service/retirement-benefits-calculator">DCRB Retirement Benefits Calculator</a>

## D.C. Worked Example Calculations

### Example 5: D.C. Teacher — 30 Years, Post-1996 Hire

> **Ms. Thompson** is a DCPS teacher hired in 2000. She retires at age 55 with 30 years of service. Her highest 36-month average salary is $116,000.

**Formula:** Average Salary × 2.0% × Years

$116,000 × 0.02 × 30 = **$69,600 per year** ($5,800/month)

That's **60% of her final average salary** — and she's not covered by Social Security, so this pension (plus any voluntary savings) is her primary retirement income.

* see also: <a href="https://katzmosesfinancial.com/dc-teacher-pension-faq/">Katz-Moses Financial — DC Teacher Pension FAQ</a>

### Example 6: D.C. Police Officer — 25 Years

> **Officer Smith** joined MPD at age 21 and retires at age 46 with 25 years of service. His highest 36-month average base pay is $100,000.

**Formula:** Average Base Pay × 2.5% × Years

$100,000 × 0.025 × 25 = **$62,500 per year** ($5,208.33/month)

That's **62.5% of his average base pay** — and he's only 46 years old. If he stays 5 more years (30 total), his pension rises to 75% of average pay. The maximum is 80% (at 32 years).

* see also: <a href="https://joinmpd.dc.gov/metropolitan-police/police-officer">JoinMPD — Retirement Benefits</a>

### Example 7: D.C. Teacher — Pre-1996 Hire, Weighted Formula

> **Mr. Garcia** was hired in 1990. He retires at age 60 with 32 years of service. His highest 36-month average salary is $105,000.

**Formula (weighted):**
- First 5 years: $105,000 × 1.5% × 5 = $7,875
- Years 5–10: $105,000 × 1.75% × 5 = $9,187.50
- Years beyond 10: $105,000 × 2.0% × 22 = $46,200

Total: $7,875 + $9,187.50 + $46,200 = **$63,262.50 per year** ($5,271.88/month)

## Key Formula Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **High-3** | The average of your highest 3 consecutive years of basic pay — the salary base for federal pension calculations |
| **Multiplier** | The percentage of salary earned per year of service (1.0%, 1.1%, 1.7%, 2.0%, or 2.5% depending on system and category) |
| **Average Salary (D.C.)** | The highest average actual rate of pay over any 36 consecutive months of service |
| **Creditable Service** | Total years and partial years of qualifying employment, including purchased service and unused sick leave |
| **Diet COLA** | The reduced COLA that FERS annuitants receive when inflation exceeds 2% |
| **Special Retirement Supplement (SRS)** | A bridge payment approximating Social Security for FERS retirees who retire before 62 with full career (MRA+30 or age 60+20) |
| **Equalization Pay** | Annual pay adjustment for D.C. Police/Fire Tier 1 retirees (retired before 2/15/1980) based on active-duty pay changes |
